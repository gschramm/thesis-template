\section{Results}

\lipsum[10]

The empirical \ac{cdf} of the residual-error distribution is

\begin{equation}
  \hat{F}(x) = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} \mathbf{1}[x_{i} \leq x],
  \label{eq:ch3-ecdf}
\end{equation}

where $\mathbf{1}[\cdot]$ is the indicator function.
Under the Gaussian assumption, \eqref{eq:ch3-ecdf} converges to the
closed-form expression

\begin{equation}
  F(x;\mu,\sigma) = \frac{1}{2}\left[
    1 + \operatorname{erf}\!\left(
      \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma\sqrt{2}}
    \right)
  \right],
  \label{eq:ch3-gaussian-cdf}
\end{equation}

with mean $\mu$ and standard deviation $\sigma$~\cite{bishop2006}.

The key quantitative results are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item The proposed method (\autoref{tab:ch2-methods}) achieved a
        \SI{15.8}{\percent} improvement in accuracy over the
        baseline.
  \item The \ac{snr} increased from \SI{12}{\decibel} to
        \SI{18}{\decibel} after preprocessing
        (see \autoref{fig:ch2-flow}).
  \item Runtime remained within the real-time budget of
        \SI{2}{\second} per sample on all test cases.
  \item The area under the \ac{roc} curve reached \num{0.93},
        compared with \num{0.81} for the baseline.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}

\lipsum[11]

The theoretical guarantee behind~\eqref{eq:ch3-gaussian-cdf}
requires the following conditions to hold:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Samples are drawn i.i.d.\ from a stationary distribution.
  \item The noise process is ergodic and zero-mean.
  \item The estimator is asymptotically unbiased and
        consistent~\cite{hochreiter1997}.
\end{enumerate}

\lipsum[12]

The learning-rate schedule in~\eqref{eq:ch2-lr} was found to reduce
the training loss by a factor of \num{3.2} compared with a fixed
rate, consistent with the findings of~\cite{hochreiter1997}.
