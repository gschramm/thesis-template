Flowcharts and processing pipelines can be drawn directly in \LaTeX{}
using the \texttt{tikz} package (\autoref{fig:ch2-flow}).
This avoids raster artefacts and keeps diagrams typographically
consistent with the surrounding text.

\section{Further equations and units}

The cross-entropy loss for a $K$-class classifier is

\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}(\boldsymbol{\theta}) =
  -\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}
    \sum_{k=1}^{K} y_{ik}\,\log\hat{p}_{ik}(\boldsymbol{\theta}),
  \label{eq:ch2-crossentropy}
\end{equation}

where $y_{ik} \in \{0,1\}$ is the ground-truth label and
$\hat{p}_{ik}$ is the predicted probability for class~$k$.
Minimising~\eqref{eq:ch2-crossentropy} via stochastic gradient
descent gives the update rule

\begin{align}
  \boldsymbol{\theta}^{(t+1)}
    &= \boldsymbol{\theta}^{(t)}
       - \eta\,\nabla_{\!\boldsymbol{\theta}}\,
         \mathcal{L}\!\left(\boldsymbol{\theta}^{(t)}\right),
       \label{eq:ch2-sgd} \\
  \eta_{t}
    &= \eta_{0}\cdot\gamma^{\lfloor t/s \rfloor},
       \label{eq:ch2-lr}
\end{align}

where $\eta_{0}$ is the initial learning rate, $\gamma < 1$ is the
decay factor, and $s$ is the step size~\cite{lecun1998}.

Use the \texttt{siunitx} package for physical quantities and units.
For example, the experiment ran for \SI{72}{\hour} on a GPU with
\SI{40}{\giga\byte} of memory, achieving an accuracy of
\SI{88.1}{\percent} after \num{50000} gradient steps.
